Technical Approach

Volumetric rendering requires solving the radiative transfer equation through a heterogeneous medium. The core components are:

  • Delta tracking (Woodcock tracking) — unbiased free-path sampling through heterogeneous volumes using a majorant extinction coefficient.
  • Henyey-Greenstein phase function — single parameter g controls forward/backward scattering anisotropy; analytically importance-sampled.
  • OpenVDB via NanoVDB — JangaFX's free CC0 EmberGen VDB assets (Grenade Dust Impact, Industrial Chimney Smoke) loaded as OptiX custom primitives.
  • Chromatic scattering — per-channel RGB scattering coefficients to produce colored smoke.
  • Volume NEE — next-event estimation extended into volumes with ratio-tracked transmittance shadow rays.

The VDB density fields come from JangaFX's free EmberGen animations, licensed CC0 (public domain).

  • Henyey & Greenstein 1941 — analytic phase function for scattering anisotropy
  • NVIDIA NanoVDB — GPU-native OpenVDB for OptiX/CUDA kernels
